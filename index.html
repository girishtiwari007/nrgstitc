⚠️ Key Ambiguity (MB Division letter 15/09/2025 + RBA 19/2024): HSN/SAC 9954 covers both immovable property (T3 — blocked) and Plant & Machinery works contracts (T4 — eligible). Finance Act 2025 Sec 124 retrospectively changes "plant or machinery" to "plant and machinery" from 01.07.2017 — nullifying Safari Retreats SC ruling for building-as-plant argument. The single deciding test: Is the asset Plant & Machinery (P&M), or is it an immovable structure?

📌 Capital vs Revenue & Deposit Works: ITC flag at IPAS must match the fund allocation. Capital works (Plan Heads) creating new assets → evaluate if P&M (T4) or immovable structure (T3). Revenue works (Demand 3–13, Working Expenses) → maintenance/repair: services → C2, materials → T3. Deposit works (third-party funded via deposit account) → same ITC rules apply based on nature of asset created.

🏗️ Capital Fund Works (Plan Heads 1100–6300)

  • New line / doubling / gauge conversion (civil) → T3
  • New station building / FOB / ROB (structure) → T3
  • New signalling / Kavach installation → T4 (P&M)
  • New OHE / traction electrification → T4 (P&M)
  • Workshop P&M / lathe / CNC installation → T4 (P&M)
  • Lifts & escalators at stations → T4 (P&M)
  • New rolling stock procurement (AC/freight) → T4
  • Staff quarters / colonies (capital) → T3
  • DRF replacement of immovable structure → T3
  • DRF replacement of P&M / rolling stock → T4

🔧 Revenue Working Expenses (Demands 3–13)

  • P.Way maintenance — service portion → C2
  • P.Way maintenance — materials/goods → T3
  • OHE / S&T repair — service → C2
  • OHE / S&T repair — goods/materials → T3
  • Loco / coach / wagon repair (workshop services) → C2
  • Workshop repair goods (stock items) → T3
  • AC coach maintenance (dedicated) → T4
  • Station cleaning / security / OBHS (common) → C2
  • Building maintenance / repair (any) → T3
  • Non-AC operations (direct exclusive inputs) → T2
  • Staff welfare / medical / school → T1

💰 Deposit Works (Third-Party Funded)

  • ROB / RUB deposit (State Govt) → T3
    Asset = immovable bridge. Railways executes as agency. T3 at IR IPAS stage.
  • Electrification deposit (State / SPV) → T4
    OHE/traction = P&M. T4 eligible if used for taxable output.
  • Signalling deposit (Kavach/IBS) → T4
    P&M equipment. Full ITC.
  • RVNL / DFCCIL / IRCON works → EX
    CBIC 228/2024: exempt from GST w.e.f. 15.07.2024. Regularised 01.07.2017. No GST on inward supply → no ITC question.
  • Ministry of Defence deposit — immovable → T3
  • Ministry of Defence deposit — P&M/equipment → T4

⚖️ Capitalised vs Revenue Repair

  • Capitalised repair (repair that increases asset life / creates new capacity, capitalised in books) → treated as construction → T3
    Finance Act 2025 Sec 124: "construction includes renovation to the extent capitalised"
  • Revenue repair (routine, not capitalised) → works contract on immovable → T3 (service); goods → T3
  • Revenue repair of P&M (service portion) → C2
  • Revenue repair of P&M (goods/materials) → T3 (RBA 19/2024)
  • New P&M under capital → T4 (exclusively taxable) or C2 (mixed AC+NonAC)

🆕 RBA 13/2026 (GST Circular 3/2026) — Demand 11 (SMH-09) Staff Welfare & Amenities
Issued by Railway Board, Director/Accounts (Megha Agrawal), June 2026. Reviews ITC eligibility for all transactions booked under erstwhile Demand 11 (SMH-09) Abstract-J — Staff Welfare and Amenities. Based on Zonal Railway feedback and RB GST consultants' opinion: ITC is GENERALLY UNAVAILABLE for Demand 11 transactions. IPAS software has been updated to enforce mandatory justification before any T4/C2 flag can be used on Demand 11 bills.

🚫 Default Treatment — T3 (No ITC)

All expenditure booked under Demand 11 (SMH-09) Abstract-J — staff welfare, recreation, amenities, canteen subsidy, sports facilities, cultural activities, welfare centres — is by default classified as T3. This is the conservative starting position adopted by Railway Board's GST consultants based on Section 17(5) of the CGST Act.

Legal basis: Section 17(5) operates as a non-obstante override — it disallows ITC on specified categories irrespective of business nexus. Most staff welfare expenditure falls within blocked categories (employee benefit, personal consumption, club/recreation facilities).

✅ Exception A — Statutory Obligation Test

ITC may be allowed (flag T4 or C2) if the expenditure is mandatory under any law.

Example: Canteen facilities where mandated by the Factories Act, 1948 (applicable to certain categories of railway establishments employing above a threshold number of workers).

Must be justified in IPAS by selecting: "Statutory obligation — expenditure mandated under applicable law"

✅ Exception B — Operational Nexus Test

ITC may be allowed (flag T4 or C2) if there is a direct and proximate connection with core railway operations, AND that ITC is not specifically barred under Section 17(5).

Example: Technical training for train operations (where the training service itself is not a blocked category under 17(5)).

Must be justified in IPAS by selecting: "Not covered under blocked credits under Section 17(5)"

📋 IPAS System Enforcement — Step-by-Step Workflow

1

User enters allocation with Demand-11 and selects ITC Flag T4 or C2

System automatically detects Demand-11 + T4/C2 combination and displays an "ITC Justification" button adjacent to the ITC Flag field on the bill-passing screen.

2

User clicks "ITC Justification" button

A popup window titled "ITC Applicability for Demand-11" opens, requiring selection of exactly ONE option:

  • ○ Not covered under blocked credits under Section 17(5)
  • ○ Statutory obligation — expenditure mandated under applicable law
  • ● Other (Specify) — requires mandatory Justification Description text + Remarks
3

System validates entry before allowing bill to proceed

Justification details must be entered and saved via the popup before the system allows further processing of the bill.

4

Bill submission BLOCKED without justification

If the user attempts to pass a bill with Demand-11 and ITC Flag T4/C2 without providing justification, the system displays a validation error and prevents bill submission entirely.

⛔ Bill rejected until justification saved
5

Bill submission allowed only after justification saved

Once the justification popup is completed and saved, the bill-passing process can continue normally. The justification record is retained for future GST tax audit reference.

🎯 Why This Matters — Compliance Benefits

  • Ensures consistency across all GSTINs of Indian Railways for Demand 11 ITC treatment
  • Prevents erroneous ITC claims that could attract interest @18% p.a. under CGST Sec 50
  • Creates an audit trail — every T4/C2 claim under Demand 11 has a recorded, system-saved justification available for future GST tax audits
  • Balances conservative compliance (default T3) with flexibility for genuine statutory/operational exceptions
  • Minimizes litigation exposure by requiring documented reasoning rather than ad-hoc flagging decisions
